    Why Halo 3?

    Now, let me make this clear. The whole Halo franchise was amazing.
    I just picked Halo 3 because it was a memorial closing of the famous trilogy.

    Halo has always had an amazing story in my eyes.
    Everything takes place in such a vast universe with so much lore stored behind it.

    Halo's Levels

    I loved Bungie's idea of game design. Each level in all three Halo's were unique.
    Nothing was ever the same, whether it be from map design to opponents.
    I think this is something very critical when creating games.
    To make each stage unique keeps the player interested and excited for what will come next.

    Lets take Halo 3 as an example.
    In the first stage, players are in a unique jungle environment and experience fighting all sorts of the basic covenant opponents.
    This is also the stage where they get to taste fighting a brute chieftain for the first time. Comparing it to the second stage, players are put into a unique UNSC base camp environment.
    Players get to experience using new equipments like bubble shields and power drains.
    They also get to fight new opponents called Drones and a variety of new types of brutes.
    This experience continues on for ALL levels in Halo and is something that just makes this game so fun to play again.

    Halo's Levels

    As mentioned, Halo's story is vast. The idea behind the trilogy was simply amazing in my eyes.
    To create two strong opposing characters and develop their stories overtime was such a facinating idea.
    Eventually in Halo 3, we would see them being forced to work together against an even bigger threat.

    In Halo 2 particularly, I loved the idea that Arbiters gameplay was purposely much more harder than Master Chiefs.
    All in the sense that, his missions were literally suppose to kill him.


    Extra features I enjoyed about Halo 3


    Easter eggs and Skulls

    I really enjoyed looking for easter eggs in Halo 3 with my brother.
    I think this really gave the game its replayability because I remember spending hours looking around for secrets and skulls in each level.
    Honestly think adding skulls to find in each level was an amazing easter egg.

    (more information about Halo Skulls)

    The reward for finding them all was also amazing (Hayabusa Armour)


    Legendary Difficulty

    This was another fun part of the game that really made it challenging.
    Just like Halo 2 and Halo 1, Legendary difficulty was hard. Extremely hard.

    Although it was an absolute pain to finish it in the hardest difficulty,
    I feel like this gamemode really forces players to play the game more slower, explore new places, and try new things.
    It also extends the gameplay time tremendously compared to how fast you can complete the campaign in easy mode.
    All in all, this feature is a great add-on for players that want to push themselves and really experience playing Halo in more depth.

    Accessibility

    Once you bought halo it was free.
    What I mean is that there are no paywalls, dlc's, loot boxes and so forth that cost extra money.
    This really let me enjoy the game to its fullest without being stopped by paywalls that often surround new games nowadays.









    Should you try Halo 3?

    For sure!
    If you are an avid gamer like me,
    I can tell you that this game was extremely well done and that you won't regret playing it.

    I would recommend starting from Halo 1 though.
    The story is amazing and should be experienced from Halo 1 to 3.

    I would also recommend playing with a friend because the story can be done in Co-op.
    Also because I really enjoyed the experience of playing the trilogy with my brother.
